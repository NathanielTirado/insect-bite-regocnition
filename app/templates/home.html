The Confusion Matrix!
A summary of prediction results on a classification problem. Higher values mean there is a higher chance of the event happening. In order for the model to reach it’s true potential, we had to work to make sure the diagonal from the top left to the bottom right values were as high as possible!
The P Curve!
The distribution of statistically significant p values for a set of independent findings and takes the shape of a diagnostic of the evidential value of that set of findings. Our P curve provides the statistical backing that our model is able to effectively detect insect bites.
The F1 Curve!
A Classification Error metric used to evaluate the classification machine learning algorithms and is useful in evaluating the prediction for binary classification of data. Although our model looks for more than two different types of insect bites, the curve still provides us with reassurance that our model training was heading on the right track.
The R curve!
The R curve shows us that our model was resilient at many different classification thresholds. No matter the confidence level, our model could correctly tell us the correct result!
The PR curve!
The PR curve is a graph with Precision values on the y-axis and Recall values on the x-axis. Our PR curve relates the information found on our P and R curves on one single graphical plane, and shows us that our model is highly resilient against false positives and false negatives!
Labels and Distributions!
This graph provides us with more information on the dataset we used. As you can see, the first graph explains why our model is so good at finding bed bugs and fire ants - we have waaay more data for it! The other graphs show the distribution of various dimensions of the image such as width and height, as well as where the insect's bounding boxes typically were!.

Insect Bite Recognition

Our Insect Bite Recognition AI will allow you to upload a picture of an insect bite, and it will then relay to you which insect it came from!

Our Purpose

With this project, we aim to develop an AI that allows the user to upload a picture of any insect bite and relate all the necessary information. Essentially, users will be able to identify the bite they have and what insect gave it to them. Ultimately, we would also like to be able to tell the user whether or not that insect could be carrying a deadly disease and offer suggestions on any possible treatment they could potentially need.
